Second challenge — resetSessionStates is called where exactly
resetSessionStates sets failedAttemptsCounter back to 0, scaffoldingLevel back to NONE, and socraticRatio back to 1.0. These are transient session states — they should not persist between sessions. But your toJSON method serializes the entire behavioral object including these transient fields, which means the DeltaProcessor will see them and potentially write them into the permanent LearningProfile.
A student who ended their last session frustrated with scaffoldingLevel HIGH will start their next session with HIGH scaffolding — even if they come back fresh the next morning. Is that your intended behavior? If not, how do you separate what is transient from what is persistent in the behavioral state before the delta write happens?

The Intent: Variables like failedAttemptsCounter, scaffoldingLevel, and socraticRatio are designed to be temporary. They measure how a student is doing right now in a specific study session. When a new session starts, they are supposed to start fresh.

The Flaw: Your toJSON method acts as a camera, taking a snapshot of your data to save it to the database permanently. Right now, your camera is capturing everything in the behavioral object, including those temporary variables.

The Consequence: Because toJSON hands those temporary variables over to the permanent database (the DeltaProcessor), the system remembers them forever. So, yesterday's temporary frustration is accidentally saved as today's permanent starting point.

Your Mission: You need to figure out how to change what gets handed over to the database so that permanent traits are saved, but temporary session data is left out or ignored.

When you think about the structure of that toJSON method, how might you change what it returns so the database only gets the data it actually needs to keep?
